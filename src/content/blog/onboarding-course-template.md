## The Complete New Employee Onboarding Course Template

Great onboarding sets new employees up for success. Poor onboarding leads to confusion, disengagement, and higher turnover. This template provides a proven structure for effective new employee onboarding eLearning.

Use this as a starting point for your own onboarding course, or share it with your eLearning developer to speed up development.

## Overview: Onboarding Course Objectives

**Primary Goal:** Help new employees feel welcomed, informed, and prepared to succeed in their role.

**Key Learning Outcomes:**
- Understand company culture, values, and mission
- Know key policies, procedures, and expectations
- Navigate systems and tools confidently
- Connect with team members and resources
- Feel excited and prepared for their role

**Recommended Duration:** 60-90 minutes (delivered over first week)  
**Delivery Method:** Self-paced eLearning with manager check-ins  
**Assessment:** Knowledge checks throughout, no high-stakes final exam

## Module 1: Welcome & Company Overview (10-15 minutes)

### Learning Objectives
By the end of this module, new employees will:
- Understand the company's mission, vision, and values
- Know key milestones in company history
- Identify what makes the company culture unique
- Feel welcomed and excited about their decision to join

### Content Structure

**1.1 Welcome Message** (2-3 minutes)
- Video from CEO or leadership team
- Personal welcome and congratulations
- What to expect from onboarding
- Encouragement and excitement for their journey

**1.2 Our Story** (3-4 minutes)
- Company founding and history
- Key milestones and achievements
- Where we are today
- Where we're headed (vision for future)
- Interactive timeline with clickable highlights

**1.3 Mission, Vision & Values** (3-4 minutes)
- Clear explanation of each
- Real examples of values in action
- How these guide daily decisions
- Interactive: Match values to scenarios

**1.4 What Makes Us Different** (2-3 minutes)
- Company culture highlights
- Employee testimonials (short video clips)
- Benefits and perks overview
- Why employees love working here

**Knowledge Check:**
- What are our core values?
- Which value resonates most with you? (reflection question)
- When was the company founded?

## Module 2: Your Role & Team (10-15 minutes)

### Learning Objectives
- Understand their specific role and responsibilities
- Know who's on their immediate team
- Identify key stakeholders they'll work with
- Understand how their role contributes to company success

### Content Structure

**2.1 Your Position Overview** (3-4 minutes)
- Role title and department
- Key responsibilities
- Success metrics and expectations
- How this role evolved (context)
- Career growth opportunities

**2.2 Meet Your Team** (4-5 minutes)
- Organizational chart (interactive)
- Direct manager introduction
- Team member profiles with photos
- Who to go to for what
- Team culture and working style

**2.3 Key Stakeholders** (2-3 minutes)
- Other departments you'll interact with
- Important relationships to build
- Cross-functional collaboration overview
- Communication channels and norms

**2.4 Your First Week Plan** (1-2 minutes)
- What to expect day-by-day
- Key meetings scheduled
- Initial projects or tasks
- Support available during transition

**Knowledge Check:**
- Who is your direct manager?
- Name two key stakeholders you'll work with
- What are your main responsibilities?

---

## Module 3: Essential Policies & Procedures (15-20 minutes)

### Learning Objectives
- Understand key company policies
- Know workplace expectations and standards
- Identify where to find policy information
- Recognize how to handle common situations

### Content Structure

**3.1 Code of Conduct** (4-5 minutes)
- Professional behavior expectations
- Ethical standards
- Conflict of interest
- Reporting concerns
- Real scenarios: "What would you do?"

**3.2 Workplace Policies** (5-6 minutes)
- Working hours and flexibility
- Remote work policy (if applicable)
- Dress code and appearance
- Breaks and meal periods
- Time off and leave policies
- Sick leave procedures

**3.3 Communication Standards** (2-3 minutes)
- Email etiquette
- Meeting norms
- Response time expectations
- Using company communication tools
- Professional social media guidelines

**3.4 Health, Safety & Wellbeing** (3-4 minutes)
- Workplace health and safety basics
- Emergency procedures
- Reporting incidents or hazards
- Mental health support available
- Wellbeing resources

**3.5 Information Security** (2-3 minutes)
- Password requirements
- Data protection basics
- Using company devices
- Email security awareness
- Who to contact for IT issues

**Knowledge Check:**
- Where can you find the full employee handbook?
- What should you do if you witness unethical behavior?
- Who do you contact to report a safety concern?

---

## Module 4: Systems & Tools (15-20 minutes)

### Learning Objectives
- Access and navigate essential systems
- Use primary tools confidently
- Know who to contact for technical support
- Complete basic tasks in key platforms

### Content Structure

**4.1 Getting Set Up** (3-4 minutes)
- Email and calendar setup
- Company intranet/portal access
- How to request IT support
- Password management
- Mobile device setup (if applicable)

**4.2 Core Systems Overview** (6-8 minutes)

*Customize this section based on your tools:*

- **HRIS/Payroll System**
  - Logging in for the first time
  - Updating personal details
  - Viewing pay slips
  - Submitting leave requests

- **Project Management Tool**
  - Accessing your team's workspace
  - Understanding project structure
  - Updating task status
  - Where to find information

- **Communication Platform** (Slack/Teams)
  - Key channels to join
  - Direct messaging etiquette
  - Status and availability
  - Notification settings

- **Document Storage** (Drive/SharePoint)
  - Finding team folders
  - Saving and organizing files
  - Sharing and permissions
  - Version control basics

**4.3 Finding Information** (2-3 minutes)
- Company wiki or knowledge base
- Department-specific resources
- Templates and forms
- Who to ask when you can't find something

**4.4 Quick Reference Guide** (Downloadable)
- System login URLs
- IT support contact
- Key shortcuts and tips
- Troubleshooting common issues

**Interactive Elements:**
- Screen recording tours of each system
- Clickable system interface practice
- "Try it yourself" simulated tasks

**Knowledge Check:**
- How do you submit a leave request?
- Where do you find team documents?
- Who do you contact for system access issues?

## Module 5: Culture & Connection (10-15 minutes)

### Learning Objectives
- Understand how to build relationships
- Know social and team-building opportunities
- Feel connected to company culture
- Identify resources for support and development

### Content Structure

**5.1 Building Relationships** (3-4 minutes)
- Importance of connections
- Coffee chat culture
- Virtual/in-person meetups
- Cross-team collaboration opportunities
- Tips for remote relationship building

**5.2 Learning & Development** (2-3 minutes)
- Training resources available
- Professional development opportunities
- Mentorship programs
- Performance review process overview
- Growth mindset culture

**5.3 Getting Involved** (2-3 minutes)
- Employee resource groups
- Social committees and events
- Volunteering opportunities
- Feedback and suggestion channels
- Innovation and idea sharing

**5.4 Support & Resources** (2-3 minutes)
- HR support available
- Mental health and EAP
- Diversity and inclusion resources
- Who to talk to about challenges
- It's okay to ask for help

**5.5 Your Onboarding Checklist** (1-2 minutes)
- Key tasks for first 30/60/90 days
- Manager check-in schedule
- How to track your progress
- Celebrating early wins

**Reflection Activity:**
- What are you most excited about?
- What questions do you still have?
- What support would be most helpful?

## Module 6: Next Steps & Resources (5-10 minutes)

### Learning Objectives
- Know immediate next steps
- Access ongoing resources
- Feel prepared for Day 1
- Understand continued onboarding support

### Content Structure

**6.1 Your First Day** (2-3 minutes)
- When and where to arrive/log in
- What to bring/prepare
- Who you'll meet first
- Schedule overview
- What to wear/expect

**6.2 First Week Milestones** (2-3 minutes)
- Key meetings and introductions
- Initial projects or tasks
- Training sessions
- Check-in points
- What success looks like

**6.3 Ongoing Resources** (1-2 minutes)
- Employee handbook link
- Policy documents
- System guides
- FAQs
- Who to contact for what

**6.4 Feedback & Questions** (1-2 minutes)
- How to provide onboarding feedback
- Who to ask questions
- Regular check-in schedule
- Open door policy

**Final Message** (1 minute)
- Congratulations on completing onboarding
- Excitement for their journey
- Reminder of support available
- Welcome to the team!

## Assessment Strategy

**Throughout Course:**
- Knowledge checks after each module (5-10 questions each)
- Low-stakes, immediate feedback
- Opportunity to review content
- Focus on understanding, not memorization

**No Final Exam Because:**
- Onboarding should feel welcoming, not stressful
- Information retention happens over time
- Manager check-ins are better assessment
- Focus is on accessibility of information

**Manager Dashboard:**
- Track completion progress
- Identify any modules not completed
- See knowledge check results
- Flag for follow-up conversations

## Design & Engagement Best Practices

### Visual Design
- Use employee photos and real workplace images
- Brand colors and logo throughout
- Clean, modern layout
- Mobile-friendly design
- Video integration where appropriate

### Engagement Elements
- Interactive organizational charts
- Clickable office/facility tours
- Employee testimonial videos
- Scenario-based decision making
- Progress tracking and gamification (optional)

### Tone & Voice
- Warm and welcoming
- Conversational, not corporate
- Encouraging and supportive
- Authentic and genuine
- Reflects company culture

### Accessibility
- Captions on all videos
- Screen reader compatible
- Color contrast standards
- Simple, clear language
- Alternative text on images

## Delivery & Implementation

### Timing
- **Pre-Day 1:** Send welcome email with course link
- **Day 1 Morning:** Modules 1-2 (culture and role)
- **Day 1 Afternoon:** Module 3 (policies)
- **Day 2:** Module 4 (systems and tools)
- **Day 3-5:** Modules 5-6 as time allows
- **Week 1:** Complete all modules

### Manager Involvement
- **Before Day 1:** Customize role-specific content
- **Day 1:** Welcome conversation, set expectations
- **Day 2-3:** Check on system access and progress
- **End Week 1:** Completion check-in, answer questions
- **30 Days:** Progress review
- **60 Days:** Mid-point check-in
- **90 Days:** Full onboarding review

### Technical Setup
- Deliver via your LMS
- SCORM tracking for completion
- Mobile-accessible
- Downloadable resources
- Print-friendly reference guides

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

❌ **Too Much Information**
Don't overwhelm with every policy detail. Link to full handbook for deep dives.

❌ **Death by PowerPoint**
Use videos, interactions, and scenarios - not just text slides.

❌ **One-Size-Fits-All**
Customize for different roles, departments, or locations.

❌ **Set It and Forget It**
Update regularly - policies change, people change, systems change.

❌ **No Human Connection**
eLearning supplements human onboarding, doesn't replace it.

❌ **Too Corporate/Boring**
Make it engaging and reflect your actual culture, not aspirational fluff.

## Measuring Success

Track these metrics:

**Completion Metrics:**
- % of new hires completing onboarding
- Average time to complete
- Module completion rates
- Drop-off points (where people stop)

**Engagement Metrics:**
- Knowledge check scores
- Video watch completion
- Interactive element usage
- Reflection question responses

**Business Metrics:**
- Time to productivity
- 30/60/90 day retention rates
- New hire satisfaction scores
- Manager satisfaction with preparedness

**Qualitative Feedback:**
- Exit surveys from onboarding
- Manager feedback
- New hire feedback in 1-on-1s
- Areas of confusion or questions
