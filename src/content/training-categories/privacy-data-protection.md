## The Problem With Most Privacy Training

Here's the uncomfortable truth: most people tune out during privacy training.

Why? Because it feels like:

- A wall of legal text about regulations
- Abstract concepts disconnected from daily work
- Someone else's problem (IT? Legal? Compliance?)
- Rules that slow down getting work done

The result? Your team knows you have a privacy policy, but they don't know what it means for the email they're about to send or the spreadsheet they're about to share.

That's when breaches happen.

## Our Approach: Make Privacy Practical, Not Theoretical

We build privacy training around the actual decisions your team makes every day:

### Real Data Scenarios

Not abstract policy language. Real situations: "Can I email this customer list to my personal account?" "Can I discuss this client on a Zoom call from the coffee shop?" "What do I do with these old files?"

### Your Actual Systems

Training that references the tools your team actually uses - your CRM, your file sharing, your communication platforms. If you use Salesforce and SharePoint, that's what we talk about.

### Role-Specific Risks

Sales teams handle data differently than HR, who handle it differently than marketing. We tailor scenarios to what each role actually encounters.

### The "So What" Factor

People protect data when they understand the impact. We connect privacy breaches to real consequences: customer trust, regulatory fines, personal liability, competitive advantage lost.

### Decision-Making Framework

Not rules to memorise, but a thinking framework: "Before I share/store/access this data, what should I consider?"

## Here's Why Custom Privacy Training Matters

Generic Training Fails Because:

- It focuses on regulations, not practical application
- Scenarios don't match how your team actually works
- It doesn't reference your specific tools and systems
- People can't connect abstract rules to daily decisions
- There's no clear "what do I do right now" guidance

Custom Training Works Because:

- Your team sees their actual work situations
- It references the systems they use every day
- Role-specific scenarios feel immediately relevant
- Decision-making becomes intuitive, not rule-following
- People understand why, not just what

## The ROI

One prevented data breach saves you from regulatory fines (up to $2.5 million under APP), reputational damage, customer loss, and remediation costs. One employee who thinks before sharing could save everything.
