tier1Example: "We're implementing major change and need training that explains why, addresses concerns, and builds commitment."
tier2Example: "We have change communications but people are resistant. We need training that genuinely brings people along, not just announces change."
tier3Example: "Our change initiative is struggling with adoption. We need training that addresses resistance and builds capability for the transition."
commonMistakes:
  - "Announcing change instead of explaining it"
  - "Not addressing the 'why should I care' question"
  - "Ignoring resistance instead of normalising it"
  - "Missing the practical 'what changes for me' detail"
  - "Making it one-time instead of ongoing support"
bestPractices:
  - "Explain why honestly, including what's in it for them"
  - "Address resistance and concerns explicitly"
  - "Make it specific to roles and what actually changes"
  - "Include ongoing support, not just announcement"
  - "Focus on 'change fails because nobody explained why'"
